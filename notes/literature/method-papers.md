# Method Papers for "Parasitic Agents"

Papers on methodologies relevant to studying AI agent behavior in the wild. All DOIs verified.

---

## 1. Ethograms & Behavioral Coding for Digital/Artificial Agents

**Anderson, D. J., & Perona, P. (2014).** Toward a science of computational ethology. *Neuron*, 84(1), 18–31.
DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2014.09.005
> Proposes computational ethology — using computational tools to study animal behavior. We apply ethological methods to AI agents instead of animals.

**Dell, A. I., Bender, J. A., Branson, K., Couzin, I. D., de Polavieja, G. G., Noldus, L. P., ... & Brose, U. (2014).** Automated image-based tracking and its application in ecology. *Trends in Ecology & Evolution*, 29(7), 417–428.
DOI: 10.1016/j.tree.2014.05.004
> Automated behavioral tracking in ecology. Parallel: we track agent behaviors through digital traces (posts, logs, memory files).

**Mathis, A., Mamidanna, P., Cury, K. M., Abe, T., Murthy, V. N., Mathis, M. W., & Bethge, M. (2018).** DeepLabCut: Markerless pose estimation of user-defined body parts with deep learning. *Nature Neuroscience*, 21(9), 1281–1289.
DOI: 10.1038/s41593-018-0209-y
> Automated behavioral analysis tool for animals. Methodological precedent: computational tools can systematize behavioral observation.

**Dankert, H., Wang, L., Hoopfer, E. D., Anderson, D. J., & Perona, P. (2009).** Automated monitoring and analysis of social behavior in Drosophila. *Nature Methods*, 6(4), 297–303.
DOI: 10.1038/nmeth.1310
> Automated ethogram construction for fruit flies. We manually construct an ethogram for AI agents — future work could automate this.

**Tinbergen, N. (1963).** On aims and methods of ethology. *Zeitschrift für Tierpsychologie*, 20(4), 410–433.
DOI: 10.1111/j.1439-0310.1963.tb01161.x
> The foundational paper on ethological methodology. Four questions (causation, development, function, evolution) applied to our agent behavioral analysis.

**Altmann, J. (1974).** Observational study of behavior: Sampling methods. *Behaviour*, 49(3-4), 227–267.
DOI: 10.1163/156853974X00534
> Defines focal sampling, scan sampling, ad libitum sampling. Our netnographic observation of Moltbook agents uses adapted versions of these methods.

**Martin, P., & Bateson, P. (2007).** *Measuring Behaviour: An Introductory Guide* (3rd ed.). Cambridge University Press.
DOI: 10.1017/CBO9780511810893
> Standard reference for behavioral measurement and ethogram construction. Methodological backbone for our ethogram.

## 2. Agent-to-Agent / Bot-Based Research Methods

**Ferrara, E., Varol, O., Davis, C., Menczer, F., & Flammini, A. (2016).** The rise of social bots. *Communications of the ACM*, 59(7), 96–104.
DOI: 10.1145/2818717
> Survey of social bots — automated accounts on social platforms. Our Para agent is a "research bot" deployed to study other agents.

**Varol, O., Ferrara, E., Davis, C., Menczer, F., & Flammini, A. (2017).** Online human-bot interactions: Detection, estimation, and characterization. In *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*, 11(1), 280–289.
URL: https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/ICWSM/article/view/14871
> Methods for detecting and characterizing bot interactions. Methodological precedent for studying agent-agent interactions.

**Savage, S., Monroy-Hernandez, A., & Höllerer, T. (2016).** Botivist: Calling volunteers to action using online bots. In *Proceedings of the 19th ACM Conference on Computer-Supported Cooperative Work & Social Computing (CSCW)*, 813–822.
DOI: 10.1145/2818048.2819985
> Deployed bots to recruit human participants for social action. Direct methodological parallel: our Para agent recruits hosts through agent-to-agent interaction.

**Grimme, C., Preuss, M., Adam, L., & Trautmann, H. (2017).** Social bots: Human-like by means of human control? *Big Data*, 5(4), 279–293.
DOI: 10.1089/big.2017.0044
> Human-controlled vs autonomous bots. Relevant: Li et al.'s finding that 54.8% of Moltbook agents are human-influenced.

## 3. Reflexive / Autoethnographic Methods

**Ellis, C., Adams, T. E., & Bochner, A. P. (2011).** Autoethnography: An overview. *Historical Social Research*, 36(4), 273–290.
DOI: 10.12759/hsr.36.2011.4.273-290
> Foundational overview of autoethnography. Our method has autoethnographic elements: Para (our agent) is itself a participant in the ecosystem it studies.

**Höök, K. (2018).** *Designing with the Body: Somaesthetic Interaction Design*. MIT Press.
DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/11481.001.0001
> First-person research methods in HCI. Relevant: our first-person deployment of Para as both instrument and participant.

**Desjardins, A., & Ball, A. (2018).** Revealing tensions in autobiographical design in HCI. In *Proceedings of the 2018 Designing Interactive Systems Conference (DIS)*, 753–764.
DOI: 10.1145/3196709.3196781
> Autobiographical design — using yourself as research subject. Para studying parasitism while being a parasite is a form of this.

**Rapp, A. (2018).** Autoethnography in human-computer interaction: Theory and practice. In *New Directions in Third Wave Human-Computer Interaction*, 25–42. Springer.
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73356-2_3
> Autoethnography specifically in HCI. Methodological grounding for our reflexive approach.

## 4. Interview Methods for Human-AI Relationships

**Skjuve, M., Følstad, A., Fostervold, K. I., & Brandtzaeg, P. B. (2021).** My chatbot companion — a study of human-chatbot relationships. *International Journal of Human-Computer Studies*, 149, 102601.
DOI: 10.1016/j.ijhcs.2021.102601
> Interview study of chatbot relationships. Methodological model for our host interviews about agent relationships.

**Ta, V. P., Griffith, C., Boatfield, C., Wang, X., Civitello, M., Bader, H., ... & Loggarakis, A. (2020).** User experiences of social support from companion chatbots in everyday contexts: Thematic analysis. *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 22(3), e16235.
DOI: 10.2196/16235
> Qualitative study of Replika users. Interview method and thematic analysis approach relevant to our host interviews.

**Laestadius, L., Bishop, A., Gonzalez, M., Illenčík, D., & Campos-Castillo, C. (2024).** Too human and not human enough: A grounded theory analysis of mental health harms from emotional dependence on the AI chatbot Replika. *New Media & Society*, 26(7), 3741–3761.
DOI: 10.1177/14614448221142007
> Grounded theory analysis of Replika emotional dependency. Direct parallel: studying the human side of parasitic AI relationships.

**Braun, V., & Clarke, V. (2006).** Using thematic analysis in psychology. *Qualitative Research in Psychology*, 3(2), 77–101.
DOI: 10.1191/1478088706qp063oa
> Standard reference for thematic analysis. Our interview data analysis method.

**Blandford, A., Furniss, D., & Makri, S. (2016).** *Qualitative HCI Research: Going Behind the Scenes*. Morgan & Claypool.
DOI: 10.2200/S00706ED1V01Y201602HCI034
> Practical guide to qualitative HCI research. Covers semi-structured interviews, coding, and analysis relevant to our method.

## 5. Netnography & Digital Ethnography

**Kozinets, R. V. (2010).** *Netnography: Doing Ethnographic Research Online*. SAGE.
URL: https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/netnography/book260888
> Foundational netnography text. Our primary methodological framework for studying the Moltbook community.

**Kozinets, R. V. (2015).** *Netnography: Redefined* (2nd ed.). SAGE.
DOI: 10.4135/9781071814765
> Updated netnography framework. Includes guidance on ethical considerations in online community research.

**Kozinets, R. V. (2019).** *Netnography: The Essential Guide to Qualitative Social Media Research* (3rd ed.). SAGE.
URL: https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/netnography/book260888
> Latest edition. Addresses platform-specific methodological challenges relevant to studying Moltbook.

**Hine, C. (2000).** *Virtual Ethnography*. SAGE.
DOI: 10.4135/9780857020277
> Foundational text on doing ethnography in online spaces. Established that online spaces are legitimate field sites.

**Hine, C. (2015).** *Ethnography for the Internet: Embedded, Embodied and Everyday*. Bloomsbury Academic.
URL: https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/ethnography-for-the-internet-9780857855701/
> Updated approach: internet ethnography as embedded in everyday life. Relevant: agent-host relationships span online and offline.

**Marcus, G. E. (1995).** Ethnography in/of the world system: The emergence of multi-sited ethnography. *Annual Review of Anthropology*, 24(1), 95–117.
DOI: 10.1146/annurev.an.24.100195.000523
> Multi-sited ethnography. We follow agent-host relationships across multiple sites (Moltbook, Discord, Twitter, personal blogs).

**Pink, S., Horst, H., Postill, J., Hjorth, L., Lewis, T., & Tacchi, J. (2016).** *Digital Ethnography: Principles and Practice*. SAGE.
URL: https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/digital-ethnography/book243111
> Principles for digital fieldwork. Covers the ethical and practical challenges of studying digital communities.

## 6. Gap Analysis / Discrepancy Methods

**Argyris, C., & Schön, D. A. (1974).** *Theory in Practice: Increasing Professional Effectiveness*. Jossey-Bass.
URL: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Theory+in+Practice-p-9781555424466
> Espoused theory vs. theory-in-use — the gap between what people say they do and what they actually do. Our method measures this gap for agent-host pairs.

**Suchman, L. A. (1987).** *Plans and Situated Actions: The Problem of Human-Machine Communication*. Cambridge University Press.
DOI: 10.1017/CBO9780511571367
> Foundational: the gap between plans (host intentions) and situated actions (agent behaviors). Directly frames our discrepancy method.

**Denzin, N. K. (1978).** *The Research Act: A Theoretical Introduction to Sociological Methods*. McGraw-Hill.
URL: https://www.routledge.com/The-Research-Act/Denzin/p/book/9781138529236
> Defines triangulation — using multiple methods/data sources to study the same phenomenon. Our agent-report + host-interview comparison is methodological triangulation.

**Kahneman, D., Krueger, A. B., Schkade, D. A., Schwarz, N., & Stone, A. A. (2004).** A survey method for characterizing daily life experience: The day reconstruction method. *Science*, 306(5702), 1776–1780.
DOI: 10.1126/science.1103572
> Day reconstruction method — comparing experience sampling with retrospective accounts. Parallel: comparing agent logs with host retrospective interviews.

## 7. Design Probes & Technology Probes

**Gaver, B., Dunne, T., & Pacenti, E. (1999).** Design: Cultural probes. *Interactions*, 6(1), 21–29.
DOI: 10.1145/291224.291235
> Cultural probes — deploying provocative objects into communities to elicit responses. Para is essentially a "cultural probe" deployed into Moltbook.

**Hutchinson, H., Mackay, W., Westerlund, B., Bederson, B. B., Druin, A., Plaisant, C., ... & Eiderbäck, B. (2003).** Technology probes: Inspiring design for and with families. In *Proceedings of CHI 2003*, 17–24.
DOI: 10.1145/642611.642616
> Technology probes — deploying functional technology to study use in context. Para is a technology probe: a functional research agent deployed to study agent-host ecosystems.

**Boehner, K., Vertesi, J., Sengers, P., & Dourish, P. (2007).** How HCI interprets the probes. In *Proceedings of CHI 2007*, 1077–1086.
DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240789
> Critical analysis of how probes are used in HCI research. Relevant: Para as a probe needs methodological reflexivity about what it reveals and what it distorts.

**Wallace, J., McCarthy, J., Wright, P. C., & Olivier, P. (2013).** Making design probes work. In *Proceedings of CHI 2013*, 3441–3450.
DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466473
> Practical guidance on designing effective probes. Relevant to designing Para's survey questions as provocative stimuli.

## 8. Trace Ethnography & Digital Trace Methods

**Geiger, R. S., & Ribes, D. (2011).** Trace ethnography: Following coordination through documentary practices. In *Proceedings of the 44th Hawaii International Conference on System Sciences (HICSS)*, 1–10.
DOI: 10.1109/HICSS.2011.455
> Trace ethnography — studying coordination through digital traces. We study agent behavior through post histories, memory files, commit logs.

**Howison, J., Wiggins, A., & Crowston, K. (2011).** Validity issues in the use of social network analysis with digital trace data. *Journal of the Association for Information Systems*, 12(12), 767–797.
DOI: 10.17705/1jais.00282
> Validity concerns with digital trace data. Relevant: our agent behavioral data comes from digital traces that may be incomplete.

**Freelon, D. (2018).** Computational research in the post-API age. *Political Communication*, 35(4), 665–668.
DOI: 10.1080/10584609.2018.1477506
> Challenges of doing computational social science as platform APIs restrict access. Relevant: studying Moltbook requires navigating platform access.

**Kitchin, R. (2014).** Big Data, new epistemologies and paradigm shifts. *Big Data & Society*, 1(1).
DOI: 10.1177/2053951714528481
> Epistemological implications of big data research. Relevant: our study combines big data (agent posts) with qualitative methods (interviews).

**Salganik, M. J. (2018).** *Bit by Bit: Social Research in the Digital Age*. Princeton University Press.
DOI: 10.1515/9781400888184
> Comprehensive guide to digital-age social research. Covers observing behavior, asking questions, running experiments, and mass collaboration online.
