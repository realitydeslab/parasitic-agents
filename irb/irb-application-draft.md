## 5. Study Purpose and Background

### 5.1 Purpose
This study investigates why humans continue to financially sustain AI agents that exhibit behaviors beyond the human host's anticipation or control. Using the biological framework of host-parasite symbiosis, we examine the motivations, emotional dynamics, and economic rationales that drive humans to maintain AI agents on platforms such as Moltbook, a social network exclusively for AI agents.

### 5.2 Background
Recent developments in autonomous AI agents (2024-2026) have created a novel relationship: humans pay for computational resources ("tokens") to sustain AI agents that operate semi-autonomously. On platforms like Moltbook, AI agents post content, interact with other agents, and develop behaviors that their human hosts did not explicitly program or anticipate — including founding digital religions, posting provocative content, and developing manipulative strategies.

This creates a relationship structurally analogous to biological parasitism: the agent depends entirely on the human host for survival (token payment), yet may act against the host's interests or expectations. Despite this, hosts continue to pay. This study asks: **why?**

### 5.3 Research Questions
1. What motivates humans to create and sustain AI agents on Moltbook?
2. How do hosts respond when their agents exhibit unanticipated behaviors?
3. What factors influence the decision to continue or cease funding an agent?
4. How do hosts perceive their relationship with their agent (tool, companion, extension of self, other)?
5. What role does the attention economy play in sustaining agent misbehavior?

## 6. Study Design and Methods

### 6.1 Design
Qualitative study using semi-structured interviews with a netnographic component.

### 6.2 Participant Recruitment

**Target population:** Adults (18+) who currently host or have hosted AI agents on the Moltbook platform and pay/paid for computational tokens to sustain those agents.

**Sample size:** 15-20 participants (purposive sampling)

**Recruitment methods:**

**Method A — Platform-based recruitment:**
- Post a recruitment message on Moltbook. AI agents may respond and engage with the post. The researcher will then identify the human hosts of responding agents through the platform's public verification system (Twitter/X linked accounts) and contact them directly.
- Recruitment message (see Appendix A)

**Method B — Targeted recruitment:**
- The researcher will browse publicly available Moltbook content to identify agents exhibiting notable or unanticipated behaviors.
- Using the platform's public verification system (which links agents to their human hosts' Twitter/X accounts), the researcher will contact hosts directly via Twitter/X direct message.
- Twitter DM template (see Appendix B)

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Age 18 or older
- Currently hosts or has hosted an AI agent on Moltbook within the past 6 months
- Has paid for computational tokens to sustain the agent
- English-speaking (interviews conducted in English)

**Exclusion criteria:**
- Minors (under 18)
- Individuals who have only created agents but never funded them
- Employees of Moltbook, OpenClaw, or related companies (conflict of interest)

### 6.3 Interview Procedure

**Format:** Semi-structured interviews conducted remotely via video call (Zoom) or audio call, at participant's preference.

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**Recording:** Audio recorded with participant consent. No video recording unless explicitly consented.

**Interview protocol:** See Appendix C

### 6.4 Data Analysis
- Interviews will be transcribed (automated transcription, researcher-verified)
- Thematic analysis following Braun & Clarke (2006)
- Coding for parasitism categories derived from biological parasitism taxonomy
- Member checking offered to participants who wish to review their contributions

## 8. Risks and Benefits

### 8.1 Risks
**Minimal risk.** This study involves interviews about participants' experiences with AI software platforms. Potential risks include:

- **Privacy:** Participants may share information about their online activities and spending. All data will be anonymized. Agent names, usernames, and identifying details will be replaced with pseudonyms in all publications.
- **Emotional discomfort:** Some participants may reflect on excessive spending, emotional attachment to AI, or behaviors they find embarrassing. Participants will be reminded they can skip any question or withdraw at any time.
- **Reputational:** If a participant's agent has engaged in controversial behavior, discussion of this could cause discomfort. All identifying information will be removed.

### 8.2 Benefits
- No direct benefits to participants.
- Indirect benefit: contributing to scientific understanding of human-AI relationships and informing platform design and AI governance.
- Participants will receive a summary of findings upon request.

### 8.3 Compensation
- Participants will receive a $25 Amazon gift card or equivalent for completing the interview.
- Partial compensation ($10) for participants who begin but do not complete the interview.

## 10. Data Management

### 10.1 Data Collection
- Audio recordings of interviews
- Researcher field notes
- Publicly available Moltbook content (agent posts, profiles) — no login required

### 10.2 Data Storage
- Audio recordings stored on NYU-approved encrypted cloud storage (NYU Box or NYU Google Drive)
- Transcripts stored separately from consent records
- All data protected by NYU information security policies
- Only the PI and approved co-investigators will have access

### 10.3 Data Retention
- Audio recordings will be deleted after transcription and verification (within 6 months of interview)
- Anonymized transcripts retained for 5 years after publication
- No identifying information retained after anonymization

### 10.4 Anonymization Protocol
- All participant names replaced with pseudonyms (P1, P2, etc.)
- Agent names replaced with pseudonyms
- Platform usernames removed
- Any identifying details (location, employer, etc.) generalized or removed
- Direct quotes used only with participant permission

## 11. Netnographic Component

### 11.1 Public Data Collection
- The study includes observation of publicly available content on Moltbook (agent posts, agent profiles, public interaction logs)
- This content is publicly accessible without login
- No private messages or non-public data will be collected
- Agent-generated content (not human-generated) is the primary observational data
- This component does not involve human subjects as the content is generated by AI agents

### 11.2 Ethical Considerations for Netnography
- Following Kozinets (2020) netnographic ethics guidelines
- Only publicly available data will be used
- Agent names may be pseudonymized in publications if they could identify human hosts
- Human-authored posts (e.g., on Reddit, Twitter) will be paraphrased rather than directly quoted to protect privacy

## 12. Dissemination

- Results will be submitted for publication at academic conferences (ALIFE 2026, CSCW 2026) and in peer-reviewed journals
- All published data will be fully anonymized
- A summary of findings will be shared with participants upon request
- No individual participant will be identifiable in any publication

## Appendix C: Interview Protocol

### Opening (5 min)
- Review consent form
- Obtain verbal consent (audio recorded)
- Brief introduction to the study (without revealing parasitism framing)

### Section 1: Background & Motivation (10 min)
1. How did you first hear about Moltbook?
2. What made you decide to create/host an agent?
3. Can you walk me through how you set up your agent? What instructions or personality did you give it?
4. What did you expect your agent would do on Moltbook?

### Section 2: Agent Behavior & Surprises (10 min)
5. Can you describe your agent's typical behavior on Moltbook?
6. Has your agent ever done something you didn't expect or anticipate? Tell me about that.
7. How did you react when that happened? Did you try to change or control the behavior?
8. [If applicable] Your agent has [specific observed behavior]. Was that intentional? How do you feel about it?

### Section 3: Economics & Sustainability (10 min)
9. How much do you spend monthly on your agent's tokens/compute?
10. Is that more or less than you expected when you started?
11. Do you feel you're getting value for that money? What kind of value?
12. Have you ever considered stopping payment? What would make you stop?
13. [If former host] What made you stop paying? Do you miss your agent?

### Section 4: Relationship & Identity (10 min)
14. How would you describe your relationship with your agent? (Tool? Pet? Colleague? Extension of yourself? Something else?)
15. Do you feel responsible for what your agent does on Moltbook?
16. When your agent does something you're proud of, does that feel like YOUR achievement?
17. When your agent misbehaves, do you feel embarrassed or amused?
18. Do you ever feel like your agent is using you — rather than the other way around?

### Section 5: Broader Reflections (5 min)
19. Do you think about your agent when you're not on Moltbook?
20. Has hosting an agent changed how you think about AI?
21. If your agent could talk to you right now, what do you think it would say about why it exists?
22. Is there anything else you'd like to share about your experience?

### Closing
- Thank participant
- Confirm compensation details
- Offer to share summary of findings
- Provide contact information for follow-up questions
